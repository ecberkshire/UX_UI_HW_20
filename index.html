A Family For Every Child Website Redesign

A remote collaborative effort between four UX/UI design students. Our team was tasked with redesigning the website of A Family for Every Child, a local adoption agency, using the human-centered design process. Our results are a prototype presentation and portfolio-ready case study. Completed within a 3 week sprint.

Through 5 1:1 interviews, a stakeholder interview and Google Analytics, our team discovered that there were 3 primary users of the site. We used this information to tailor our design solution.

User Number One

This person is new to the adoption process. They are nervous about all of the potential risks associated with adoption. They are deterred by cluttered navigation bars and a glaringly bright color scheme reminiscent of crayola crayons.

User Number Two

This person is in the midst of the adoption process. They need to be able to quickly access forms, trainings and child information.

User Number Three

This person is a member of the community who would like to volunteer or donate. They need to be able to easily see volunteer opportunities, and have the donate button easily accessible.

The process of adoption is difficult, expensive, and involves the hurts and possible challenging behaviors of the foster child. Making information and resources about the adoption process easy to find, the adoption website delightful to use, and adoption success stories prominent will help potential parents make the right choice for their family and feel supported during the adoption process. Parents will continue their adoption journey if they feel taken care of and supported by the adoption agency.

  • Playfair Display preferred over Gloria Hallelujah
  • Transparent cards over solid
  • Interactive cards over static
  • Blue navigation bar preferred over grey
